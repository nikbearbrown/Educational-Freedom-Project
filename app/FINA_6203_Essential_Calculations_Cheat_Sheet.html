Return Calculations

Holding Period Return (HPR)
Calculates the total return on an investment over a specific period
Input: Beginning price (P₀), Ending price (P₁), Dividends (D₁)
Output: Percentage return
Equation: R = (P₁ - P₀ + D₁) / P₀
Excel: =(EndPrice-StartPrice+Dividend)/StartPrice
Example: Stock bought at $50, sold at $55, paid $2 dividend
HPR = (55 - 50 + 2) / 50 = 7 / 50 = 0.14 or 14%
Arithmetic Average Return
Simple average of periodic returns
Input: Series of returns (R₁, R₂, ..., Rₙ)
Output: Average return
Equation: R̄ = (R₁ + R₂ + ... + Rₙ) / n
Excel: =AVERAGE(R1:Rn)
Example: Returns of 10%, 15%, -5%, 8% over 4 years
R̄ = (10% + 15% + (-5%) + 8%) / 4 = 28% / 4 = 7%
Geometric Average Return
Compound average growth rate that accounts for compounding
Input: Series of returns (R₁, R₂, ..., Rₙ)
Output: Compounded average return
Equation: Rg = [(1+R₁) × (1+R₂) × ... × (1+Rₙ)]^(1/n) - 1
Excel: =(PRODUCT(1+R1,1+R2,...,1+Rn))^(1/COUNT(R1:Rn))-1
Example: Returns of 10%, 15%, -5%, 8% over 4 years
Rg = [(1.10) × (1.15) × (0.95) × (1.08)]^(1/4) - 1
Rg = [1.291]^(1/4) - 1 = 1.066 - 1 = 0.066 or 6.6%
Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)
Annualized growth rate over multiple years
Input: Present value (PV), Future value (FV), Number of years (n)
Output: Annual growth rate
Equation: CAGR = (FV/PV)^(1/n) - 1
Excel: =(FV/PV)^(1/Years)-1 or =RRI(Years,PV,FV)
Example: Investment grows from $10,000 to $14,802 over 4 years
CAGR = ($14,802/$10,000)^(1/4) - 1 = (1.4802)^(1/4) - 1 = 1.1032 - 1 = 0.1032 or 10.32%
Monthly Return from Total Return
Converts total return into equivalent monthly return
Input: Total return (R), Number of months (n)
Output: Monthly return
Equation: rm = (1+R)^(1/n) - 1
Excel: =(1+TotalReturn)^(1/Months)-1
Example: Total return of 16% over 12 months
rm = (1+0.16)^(1/12) - 1 = (1.16)^(1/12) - 1 = 1.0124 - 1 = 0.0124 or 1.24% per month
Annualized Return
Converts shorter period return to annual equivalent
Input: Period return (R), Period length in months (n)
Output: Annual return
Equation: ra = (1+R)^(12/n) - 1
Excel: =(1+PeriodReturn)^(12/Months)-1
Example: 3% return over 3 months
ra = (1+0.03)^(12/3) - 1 = (1.03)^4 - 1 = 1.1255 - 1 = 0.1255 or 12.55% annually

Risk Measures

Standard Deviation
Measures volatility/risk of returns
Input: Series of returns (R₁, R₂, ..., Rₙ), Mean return (R̄)
Output: Standard deviation
Equation: σ = √[Σ(Ri - R̄)² / (n-1)]
Excel: =STDEV.S(R1:Rn)
Example: Returns of 10%, 15%, -5%, 8% with mean return of 7%
σ = √[((0.10-0.07)² + (0.15-0.07)² + (-0.05-0.07)² + (0.08-0.07)²) / (4-1)]
σ = √[(0.0009 + 0.0064 + 0.0144 + 0.0001) / 3] = √[0.0218/3] = √0.00727 = 0.0853 or 8.53%
Risk Premium
Additional return expected for bearing risk
Input: Expected return on asset, Risk-free rate
Output: Risk premium
Equation: RP = Expected Return - Risk-Free Rate
Excel: =ExpectedReturn-RiskFreeRate
Example: Stock with expected return of 11%, risk-free rate of 3%
RP = 11% - 3% = 8%
Excess Return
Actual return above risk-free rate
Input: Asset return, Risk-free rate
Output: Excess return
Equation: ER = Asset Return - Risk-Free Rate
Excel: =AssetReturn-RiskFreeRate
Example: Stock returned 9.5%, risk-free rate was 2.5%
ER = 9.5% - 2.5% = 7%
Probability of Loss (Normal Distribution)
Probability of negative return assuming normal distribution
Input: Expected return (μ), Standard deviation (σ)
Output: Probability
Equation: Uses Z-score: Z = (0 - μ) / σ then finds P(Z < calculated Z)
Excel: =NORM.DIST(0,ExpectedReturn,StandardDeviation,TRUE)
Example: Stock with expected return of 8% and standard deviation of 20%
Z = (0 - 0.08) / 0.20 = -0.4
P(Z < -0.4) = 0.3446 or 34.46% probability of loss

Interest Rate Calculations

Effective Annual Rate (EAR)
True annual rate accounting for compounding
Input: Annual percentage rate (APR), Compounding frequency (m)
Output: Effective annual rate
Equation: EAR = (1 + APR/m)^m - 1
Excel: =(1+APR/m)^m-1
Example: APR of 6% compounded quarterly
EAR = (1 + 0.06/4)^4 - 1 = (1 + 0.015)^4 - 1 = (1.015)^4 - 1 = 1.0614 - 1 = 0.0614 or 6.14%

EAR with Specific Compounding:

  • Annual: =(1+APR/1)^1-1 or simply =APR
  • Semiannual: =(1+APR/2)^2-1
  • Quarterly: =(1+APR/4)^4-1
  • Monthly: =(1+APR/12)^12-1
  • Weekly: =(1+APR/52)^52-1
  • Daily: =(1+APR/365)^365-1
  • Continuous: =EXP(APR)-1
Example: APR of 5% with different compounding frequencies
- Annual: (1+0.05/1)^1-1 = 5.00%
- Quarterly: (1+0.05/4)^4-1 = 5.09%
- Monthly: (1+0.05/12)^12-1 = 5.12%
- Continuous: e^0.05-1 = 5.13%

Bond Calculations

Bond Price (Basic)
Calculates present value of bond cash flows
Input: Coupon rate (c), Face value (F), Yield to maturity (r), Periods (n)
Output: Bond price
Equation: Price = Σ[C/(1+r)^t] + [F/(1+r)^n]
Excel: =PV(yield,periods,-coupon*face_value,face_value)
Example: $1,000 bond with 6% annual coupon, 5 years to maturity, YTM of 5%
C = $60 per year, F = $1,000, r = 5%, n = 5
Price = $60/(1.05)¹ + $60/(1.05)² + $60/(1.05)³ + $60/(1.05)⁴ + $60/(1.05)⁵ + $1,000/(1.05)⁵
Price = $57.14 + $54.42 + $51.83 + $49.36 + $47.01 + $783.53 = $1,043.29
Bond Price (Semiannual Payments)
Bond price with semiannual compounding
Input: Annual coupon rate (c), Face value (F), Annual YTM (r), Years to maturity (n)
Output: Bond price
Equation: Price = Σ[C/2/(1+r/2)^t] + [F/(1+r/2)^(2n)]
Excel: =PV(yield/2,years*2,-coupon*face_value/2,face_value)
Example: $1,000 bond with 6% annual coupon (3% semiannual), 5 years to maturity, YTM of 5%
C/2 = $30 per period, F = $1,000, r/2 = 2.5%, 2n = 10 periods
Price = $30/(1.025)¹ + $30/(1.025)² + ... + $30/(1.025)¹⁰ + $1,000/(1.025)¹⁰
Price = $29.27 + $28.56 + ... + $23.53 + $784.38 = $1,044.32
Current Yield
Annual coupon payment relative to current price
Input: Annual coupon payment, Current bond price
Output: Current yield percentage
Equation: Current Yield = Annual Coupon Payment / Current Price
Excel: =AnnualCoupon/CurrentPrice
Example: Bond with $80 annual coupon trading at $950
Current Yield = $80 / $950 = 0.0842 or 8.42%
Accrued Interest
Interest earned but not yet paid
Input: Coupon payment, Days since last payment, Days in period
Output: Accrued interest amount
Equation: AI = Coupon × (Days since last payment / Days in period)
Excel: =Coupon*(DaysSincePayment/DaysInPeriod)
Example: Bond with $50 semiannual coupon, 60 days since last payment, 182 days in period
AI = $50 × (60 / 182) = $50 × 0.3297 = $16.49
Invoice Price
Total price including accrued interest
Input: Clean price, Accrued interest
Output: Invoice price
Equation: Invoice Price = Clean Price + Accrued Interest
Excel: =CleanPrice+AccruedInterest
Example: Bond with clean price of $1,025 and accrued interest of $16.49
Invoice Price = $1,025 + $16.49 = $1,041.49
Zero-Coupon Bond Value
Present value of a zero-coupon bond
Input: Face value, Yield to maturity (YTM), Years to maturity
Output: Bond value
Equation: Value = Face Value / (1 + YTM)^Years
Excel: =FaceValue/(1+YTM)^Years
Example: $1,000 zero-coupon bond, 6% YTM, 10 years to maturity
Value = $1,000 / (1 + 0.06)^10 = $1,000 / 1.7908 = $558.39

Fund Management

Net Asset Value (NAV)
Per-share value of a fund
Input: Total assets, Total liabilities, Number of shares outstanding
Output: NAV per share
Equation: NAV = (Total Assets - Total Liabilities) / Shares Outstanding
Excel: =(TotalAssets-Liabilities)/SharesOutstanding
Example: Fund with $250 million in assets, $10 million in liabilities, 12 million shares outstanding
NAV = ($250,000,000 - $10,000,000) / 12,000,000 = $240,000,000 / 12,000,000 = $20 per share
Front-End Load
Sales charge when purchasing fund shares
Input: Offering price, NAV
Output: Load percentage
Equation: Load = (Offering Price - NAV) / Offering Price
Excel: =(OfferingPrice-NAV)/OfferingPrice
Example: Fund with NAV of $18.50 and offering price of $20.00
Load = ($20.00 - $18.50) / $20.00 = $1.50 / $20.00 = 0.075 or 7.5%
Portfolio Turnover
Measure of trading activity in a fund
Input: Purchases, Sales, Average portfolio value
Output: Turnover ratio
Equation: Turnover = Min(Purchases, Sales) / Average Portfolio Value
Excel: =MIN(Purchases,Sales)/AveragePortfolioValue
Example: Fund with $120 million in purchases, $100 million in sales, average portfolio value of $500 million
Turnover = Min($120M, $100M) / $500M = $100M / $500M = 0.20 or 20%
Net Return with Expenses
Return after accounting for fund expenses
Input: Gross return, Expense ratio, Other fees (e.g., 12b-1)
Output: Net return
Equation: Net Return = Gross Return - Expense Ratio - Other Fees
Excel: =GrossReturn-ExpenseRatio-OtherFees
Example: Fund with gross return of 12%, expense ratio of 1.2%, 12b-1 fee of 0.25%
Net Return = 12% - 1.2% - 0.25% = 10.55%
Holding Period Return with Fees
Multi-year return accounting for all fees
Input: Front-end load, Annual return (r), Expenses, Years
Output: Net holding period return
Equation: Net HPR = (1 - Front Load) × [(1 + r) × (1 - Expenses)]^Years - 1
Excel: =(1-FrontLoad)*((1+r)*(1-Expenses))^Years-1
Example: Fund with 5% front-end load, 10% annual gross return, 1.5% expenses, 5-year holding period
Net HPR = (1 - 0.05) × [(1 + 0.10) × (1 - 0.015)]^5 - 1
Net HPR = 0.95 × [1.10 × 0.985]^5 - 1 = 0.95 × [1.0835]^5 - 1 = 0.95 × 1.4951 - 1 = 1.4203 - 1 = 0.4203 or 42.03%

Margin Trading

Initial Margin Requirement
Minimum percentage of funds investor must provide
Equation: Initial Margin = (Investor's Deposit / Total Purchase Value) × 100%
Excel: =Deposit/(SharePrice*NumberOfShares)
Borrowed Amount
Amount financed by broker
Equation: Borrowed = Total Purchase Value × (1 - Initial Margin)
Excel: =TotalValue*(1-InitialMargin)
Current Margin Percentage
Current equity as percentage of position value
Equation: Margin % = (Current Market Value - Loan) / Current Market Value
Excel: =(CurrentPrice*Shares-LoanAmount)/(CurrentPrice*Shares)
Margin Call Price
Price that triggers a margin call
Equation: Price = Loan / [Shares × (1 - Maintenance Margin)]
Excel: =LoanAmount/(Shares*(1-MaintenanceMargin))
Effective Annual Return on Margin
Annualized return on margin investment
Equation: Annual Return = [(Final Equity / Initial Equity)^(12/months)] - 1
Excel: =((FinalEquity/InitialEquity)^(12/Months))-1

Short Selling

Initial Margin for Short Sale
Funds required to initiate short position
Equation: Required Margin = Share Price × Number of Shares × Initial Margin Rate
Excel: =SharePrice*NumberOfShares*InitialMarginRate
Short Position Margin Percentage
Current equity percentage in short position
Equation: Margin % = (Initial Margin - (Current Price - Initial Price) × Shares) / (Current Price × Shares)
Excel: =(InitialMargin-(CurrentPrice-InitialPrice)*Shares)/(CurrentPrice*Shares)
Short Sale Return
Return on short sale
Equation: Return = ((Initial Price - Ending Price) × Shares) / Initial Margin
Excel: =((InitialPrice-EndingPrice)*Shares)/InitialMargin
Short Sale Margin Call Price
Price that triggers a short margin call
Equation: Solve for P: (Initial Margin - (P - Initial Price) × Shares) / (P × Shares) = Maintenance Margin
Excel: Complex formula, often easier to use solver

Options

Call Option Payoff
Payoff at expiration for call option
Equation: Payoff = Max(Stock Price - Strike Price, 0)
Excel: =MAX(StockPrice-StrikePrice,0)
Put Option Payoff
Payoff at expiration for put option
Equation: Payoff = Max(Strike Price - Stock Price, 0)
Excel: =MAX(StrikePrice-StockPrice,0)
Call Option Profit
Profit at expiration for call option buyer
Equation: Profit = Max(Stock Price - Strike Price, 0) - Premium
Excel: =MAX(StockPrice-StrikePrice,0)-Premium
Put Option Profit
Profit at expiration for put option buyer
Equation: Profit = Max(Strike Price - Stock Price, 0) - Premium
Excel: =MAX(StrikePrice-StockPrice,0)-Premium
Short Call Profit
Profit at expiration for call option seller
Equation: Profit = Premium - Max(Stock Price - Strike Price, 0)
Excel: =Premium-MAX(StockPrice-StrikePrice,0)
Short Put Profit
Profit at expiration for put option seller
Equation: Profit = Premium - Max(Strike Price - Stock Price, 0)
Excel: =Premium-MAX(StrikePrice-StockPrice,0)
Covered Call Profit
Profit for combined stock and short call position
Equation: Profit = (Min(Current Stock Price, Strike Price) - Initial Stock Price) + Premium
Excel: =(MIN(StockPrice,StrikePrice)-InitialStockPrice)+Premium

Portfolio Management

Portfolio Return
Weighted average return of portfolio components
Equation: Rp = w₁R₁ + w₂R₂ + ... + wₙRₙ
Excel: =SUMPRODUCT(Weights,Returns)
Portfolio Rebalancing Return
Return when portfolio is rebalanced to original weights
Equation: Same as portfolio return, but weights are reset periodically
Excel: =SUMPRODUCT(OriginalWeights,PeriodReturns)
Total Portfolio Return (Without Rebalancing)
Calculates total return when weights drift with performance
Equation: Total Return = (Sum of Final Values / Initial Investment) - 1
Excel: =(SUM(FinalValues)/InitialInvestment)-1

Key Financial Terms

Financial Assets & Securities

Term Definition
Financial Assets Claims on future cash flows; includes stocks, bonds, and derivatives (not physical/real assets like machines)
Real Assets Tangible assets like equipment, real estate, or physical goods
Intangible Assets Non-physical assets that lack physical substance (patents, trademarks, etc.)
Primary Market Market where new securities are issued and sold to investors for the first time
Secondary Market Market where existing securities are bought and sold among investors
IPO Initial Public Offering: First-time sale of stock by a company to the public
SEO Seasoned Equity Offering: Additional share issuance by a company that is already publicly traded

Investment Companies & Funds

Term Definition
Investment Company Company that invests money raised from investors in a variety of securities
Mutual Fund Open-end investment company that pools investors' money to invest in securities
Closed-End Fund Investment company that issues a fixed number of shares; doesn't redeem/issue new shares; price can differ from NAV
Open-End Fund Investment company that stands ready to issue and redeem shares at NAV
Net Asset Value (NAV) (Total assets - Liabilities) ÷ Number of shares outstanding
Front-End Load Sales charge paid when purchasing fund shares
12b-1 Fee Annual marketing/distribution fee charged by some mutual funds
Expense Ratio Annual fee charged by funds to cover operating expenses, expressed as percentage of assets
Portfolio Turnover Measure of trading activity: Min(Purchases, Sales) ÷ Average portfolio value

Risk & Return Concepts

Term Definition
Risk-Return Tradeoff Higher risk investments typically offer higher potential returns
Risk Premium Difference between expected return on a risky asset and the risk-free rate; compensation for bearing risk
Excess Return Actual return on an asset minus the risk-free rate
Arithmetic Average Return Simple average of periodic returns: (R₁ + R₂ + ... + Rₙ) ÷ n
Geometric Average Return Compound average growth rate: [(1+R₁) × (1+R₂) × ... × (1+Rₙ)]^(1/n) - 1
Standard Deviation Measure of volatility/risk; square root of variance
Holding Period Return (HPR) Total return over investment period: (P₁ - P₀ + D₁) ÷ P₀

Interest Rates & Time Value

Term Definition
Annual Percentage Rate (APR) Quoted annual interest rate without accounting for compounding
Effective Annual Rate (EAR) Annual rate that accounts for compounding: (1 + APR/m)^m - 1
Zero-Coupon Bond Bond issued at a discount that makes no regular interest payments
Discount Bond Bond trading at a price below its par value
Par Value/Face Value Amount to be repaid at a bond's maturity
Callable Bond Bond that can be repurchased by the issuer before maturity
Coupon Rate Annual interest rate paid by a bond based on its face value
Yield to Maturity (YTM) Internal rate of return on a bond held to maturity
Current Yield Annual coupon payment ÷ Current market price

Trading Mechanisms

Term Definition
Market Order Order to buy/sell immediately at the current market price
Limit Order Order to buy/sell at a specified price or better (execution uncertainty, price certainty)
Buying on Margin Purchasing securities using both investor funds and funds borrowed from a broker
Initial Margin Minimum percentage of purchase price investor must deposit (set by Federal Reserve)
Maintenance Margin Minimum percentage of equity that must be maintained in a margin account
Margin Call Broker's demand for additional funds when account falls below maintenance margin
Short Selling Sale of borrowed securities with the expectation of buying them back at a lower price

Derivatives

Term Definition
Derivative Security whose value is derived from the value of an underlying asset
Call Option Right (not obligation) to buy the underlying asset at the strike price
Put Option Right (not obligation) to sell the underlying asset at the strike price
Strike/Exercise Price Price at which option holder can buy/sell the underlying asset
European Option Option that can only be exercised at expiration
American Option Option that can be exercised any time up to and including expiration
In-the-Money Call: Strike < Market price; Put: Strike > Market price
At-the-Money Strike price equals market price
Out-of-the-Money Call: Strike > Market price; Put: Strike < Market price
